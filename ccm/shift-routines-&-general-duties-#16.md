# 🛠️ CCM Shift Duties & General Duties (Procedure #16)

Your basic "rule of thumb" is that the entire mechanical and boiler room is your
"realm". You must assess the plant **twice per 12-hour shift**, with all
anomalies reported in your log book entry.

## 📅 Shift Assessment Schedule

- **First Assessment:** Must commence **within the first hour of the shift**.
  Perform your chemical treatment analysis and add feedwater chemicals during
  this round.

- **Second Assessment:** Conducted later in the shift with a specific focus on
  assessing all **Technical Safety BC (TSBC) BPVR equipment** in the facility.

---

## 🔍 Twice-Per-Shift Check Parameters

### 💨 Pressures:

- [ ] **Boilers**: B1 (Clayton) and B2 (Cleaver-Brooks) boiler pressures should
      normally read **110-120 psi**.
- [ ] **Steam Header**: The steam header pressure should be **100-110 psi**.
- [ ] **Gas Train**: Ensure **5-7 psi** upstream of regulator, and **1 psi**
      downstream.
- [ ] **Booster Pumps**: Verify the booster pump discharge pressure is **10-20
      psi**. Typical operating pressure is around **12 psi**.

### 🌡️ Temperatures & Levels:

- [ ] **Hotwell temperature**: Normally **190-200°F** to prevent boiling off
      treatment amines.
- [ ] **Hotwell water level**: Normally **50-75%** of the gauge glass.

### 📊 Boiler Outputs & Auxiliaries:

- [ ] **Stack Temperature & Flame**: Ensure the stack temperature is within
      normal operating range, and the flame is stable with no abnormal cycling.
- [ ] **Feedwater Pumps**: Verify the feedwater pumps are operating normally,
      with no unusual noise or vibration.
- [ ] **Water Softeners**: Check the softener integrator is counting down
      correctly (starts at **48,000 US gallons**), and verify the salt bed is at
      **one-third of the height of the tank**.
- [ ] **BMS Workstation**: Log into the BMS workstation and check for any alarms
      or abnormal readings. Ensure the DDC graphics are actively updating and
      matching your readings.
- [ ] **Hot-Water Boilers**: Confirm hot-water boilers are operating normally
      with no error messages or system leaks.
- [ ] **Vents & Leaks**: Check for abnormal steam discharge from the steam
      vents, and scan for leaks in the plant.

## 🔧 Once-Per-Shift Maintenance Tasks

- [ ] Blowdown the hotwel gauge glass.
- [ ] Blowdown the booster pump strainers.
- [ ] Blowdown the hotwell float chamber.
- [ ] Conduct a Cleaver-Brooks mud drum blowdown (Procedure #8).
- [ ] Test manual LWCO devices (Procedure #15).
- [ ] Test boiler feedwater, adjust rates, batch chemicals and record results.
- [ ] Check the oil level on the Clayton feedwater pump using the float-operated
      switch.
- [ ] Record the gas meter reading from the LCD integrator in the outdoor
      compound.
- [ ] Walkthrough and assess HVAC systems, cooling tower, hot water systems, DHW
      heat exchangers, air compressors, and steam traps.

## 📡 Crucial Remote Monitoring Check (Must do!)

To ensure the plant does not have to be manned 24/7:

1. **Graphics Check**: Contact the Operations Centre to ensure DDC graphics are
   actively updating and matching yours.

2. **Strobe Check**: **Trip a boiler** (silence the alarm locally, but _do not
   acknowledge it_), and have the Operations Centre confirm their strobe is
   flashing.

3. **Camera Check**: Have them confirm the camera system is live and updating.

### 🚨 CRITICAL RED LINE:

If any of these three remote checks fail, the plant **MUST** be manned 24/7 by a
minimum 4th Class Power Engineer until the issue is resolved.

## ⚠️ Essential Safety & Escalation Rules

- **No Unauthorized Changes:** **DO NOT** change any set point parameters
  without the prior approval of the People and Process Manager, Mechanical
  Operations.

- **Reporting Abnormalities:** All abnormal conditions (leaks, excessive
  equipment vibration, etc.) must be made safe, logged, and **emailed to the
  Chief Engineer**[cite: 3619, 3620].

- **Alarm Response Timing:** Steam header pressure decays rapidly. An outage of
  just 5 minutes can cause a loss of service for critical facilities. Your alarm
  response time **must be less than 30 minutes**.
