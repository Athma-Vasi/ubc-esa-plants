# CCM Shift Duties & General Duties (Procedure #16)

Your basic "rule of thumb" is that the entire mechanical and boiler room is your
realm. You must assess the plant twice per 12-hour shift, with the first round
beginning with the start of your shift. The second round should be completed
before the end of your shift.

## Twice-Per-Shift Check Parameters

- #### Pressures:

B1(Clayton) and B2(Cleaver Brooks) boiler pressures should normally read
**110-120 psi**. The steam header pressure should be **100-110 psi**.

- #### Temperatures & Levels:

Check the hotwell temperature (normally **190-200°F** to prevent boiling off
treatment amines). Check the hotwell water level (normally **50-75%** of the
gauge glass). The hotwell water level should never be below 50% of the gauge
glass.

- #### Boiler Outputs:

Record stack temperatures and monitor flame signals (normally **91-96%**).

- #### Gas Pressures:

Ensure **5-7 psi** upstream of regulator, and **1 psi** downstream.

- #### Water Softeners:

Verify the softener integrator is counting down correctly (starts at **48,000 US
gallons**). Check the softener brine tank level (normally **50-75%** of the
tank).

- #### Vents:

Check for abnormal steam discharge from the steam vents.

## Once-Per-Shift Maintenance Tasks

- Perform blowdowns on the hotwell gauge glass, booster pump strainers, and
  hotwell float chamber.

- Conduct a Cleaver-Brooks mud drum blowdown (Procedure #8).

- Check the crankcase oil level on the Clayton feedwater pump using the float
  switch.

- Test manual LWCO devices (Procedure #15).

- Perform chemical testing, adjust feed rates, and batch treatment chemicals

- Log the gas meter reading from the LCD integrator in the outdoor compound.

## Crucial Remote Monitoring Check (Must do!)

To ensure the plant does not have to be manned 24/7:

1. Contact the Operations Centre to ensure DDC graphics are actively updating
   and matching yours.

2. **Trip a boiler** (silence the alarm locally, but _do not acknowledge it_),
   and have the Operations Centre confirm their strobe is flashing.

3. Have them confirm the camera system is live and updating.

### 🚨 CRITICAL RED LINE:

If any of these three remote checks fail, the plant **MUST** be manned 24/7 by a
minimum 4th Class Power Engineer until the issue is resolved.
