# 🛠️ CCM Daily Shift Walkthrough Checklist

When you are on shift, you are responsible not just for the steam boilers, but
for all auxiliary systems in the plant. Use your four senses (sight, touch,
smell and hearing) to perform these daily checks.

## 🚪 Phase 0: Pre-Walk Safety Gate

- [ ] **PPE check complete**: (eye protection, hearing protection, leather &
      chemical-proof gloves, long-sleeved shirt, long pants, and CSA-approved
      safety boots).
- [ ] **Communications**: Radio/phone check complete and reachable by
      operations.
- [ ] **Equipment**: Flashlight available for low-light inspection points.
- [ ] **Permits & LOTO**: Review active permits and LOTO boundaries before
      entering work areas.
- [ ] **Egress**: Confirm emergency egress routes are clear before starting
      rounds.

---

## ⚡ Phase 1: Steam Boiler Room Walkthrough

- [ ] **Review the Log Book:** Check past entries at the desk by the DDC station
      for equipment issues, lockouts or water test trends.
- [ ] **Boiler Pressures:** Verify B1 (Clayton) and B2 (C.B.) operating
      pressures are normally **110–120 PSI**.
- [ ] **Steam Header Pressure:** Verify header pressure is normally **100–110
      PSI**.
- [ ] **Hotwell Check:** Verify water level is **at least 50%** of the gauge
      glass.
- [ ] Verify temperature is normally **190–200°F** _(max 200°F to prevent
      boiling off amines)_.
- [ ] **Boiler Outputs:** Record stack temperatures and monitor flame signals
      (normally **91–96%**).
- [ ] **Gas Train Pressure:** Confirm gas pressure is **5–7 PSI upstream** of
      the regulator, and **1 PSI downstream**.
- [ ] **Boiler Feedwater Pumps:** Inspect for normal operation, abnormal noise,
      or vibration.
- [ ] **Water Softener Check:** Verify the softener integrator is counting down
      correctly from **48,000 US gallons**, and the brine tank salt bed is at
      **one-third of the tank height**.
- [ ] **Steam Vents:** Scan roof-level vents to ensure there isn't an abnormal
      amount of steam escaping.
- [ ] **BMS workstation:** Log on to the CCM Plant BMS workstation and assess
      all plant conditions.
- [ ] **Scan for Physical Anomalies:** Check the room's general condition,
      housekeeping, and inspect for steam/feedwater/condensate leaks or
      excessive motor vibration.

---

## ⚙️ Phase 2: Auxiliary Systems Walkthrough

- [ ] **Upper Mezzanine:** Check the heat pumps, compressed air, and
      vacuum/suction air systems. Verify cooling tower chemical supplies.
- [ ] **Cooling Tower & Fire Systems:** Check the Motor Control Cabinet (MCC)
      for abnormal breaker positions.
- [ ] Inspect cooling tower water pumps for leaks, bearing vibration, and high
      bearing temperatures.
- [ ] Visually verify fire system pressures and ensure the raw water backflow
      preventer is not leaking.
- [ ] **Hot Water Boiler Room:** Confirm hot water boilers are operating
      normally with no error messages or leaks. Ensure they are **actively
      running when the C.B. Boiler is in operation**.
- [ ] **Emergency Generator Room:** Inspect the 660 kW emergency generator for
      fuel, oil, or coolant leaks. Check the starter-motor battery banks for
      corrosion or wear.
- [ ] **Housekeeping:** Confirm no trip/slip hazards exist, room drains are
      clear, and access to all emergency equipment remains unobstructed.

---

## 🔧 Phase 3: Escalation and Handoff

### Escalation Triggers (Call/Notify Immediately)

- [ ] Uncontrolled fuel, steam, water, oil, or coolant leak.
- [ ] Boiler flame instability, repeated burner trip, or lockout.
- [ ] Critical alarm on DDC/MCC that does not clear after first check.
- [ ] Fire system pressure abnormal or emergency system unavailable.
- [ ] Generator support systems unsafe or degraded.
